Welcome to Radix Sort Visualizer!

Radix Sort sorts numbers digit by digit, from the least significant digit (LSD) to the most significant digit (MSD).

Unlike comparison-based sorts, it uses bucket distribution for each digit position.

Pseudocode
radixSort(arr):
max = findMax(arr)
exp = 1
while max / exp > 0:
countingSort(arr, exp)
exp *= 10
countingSort(arr, exp):
buckets = [[], [], ...] // 10
for num in arr:
digit = (num / exp) % 10
buckets[digit].append(num)
arr = flatten(buckets)

About Radix Sort

Radix Sort is a non-comparative sorting algorithm that processes numbers digit by digit.

LSD First: Start from least significant digit

Buckets: Distribute into 10 buckets (0-9)

Collect: Gather back in bucket order

Repeat: Process next digit position

Radix Sort can be faster than O(n log n) comparison sorts for integers with a fixed number of digits, making it ideal for sorting large datasets of numbers.
