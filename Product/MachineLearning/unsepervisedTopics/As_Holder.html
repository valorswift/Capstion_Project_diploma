Understanding Association Analysis

Association analysis is an unsupervised data mining technique that discovers relationships between variables or items in datasets. Unlike supervised learning, it works without labeled outcomes—instead finding hidden patterns in transactional data.

The goal is to generate association rules showing how items are related. These rules reveal patterns such as "customers who buy X also tend to buy Y", enabling data-driven decisions in retail, e-commerce, and beyond.

Association Analysis Guide

What is Association Analysis?

Association analysis is an unsupervised data mining technique that discovers relationships between items in large datasets. It finds patterns like "customers who buy X also buy Y" without predefined labels.

Understanding Association Rules

  • Antecedent - The "if" part of a rule (item A)
  • Consequent - The "then" part of a rule (item B)
  • Rules reveal hidden patterns in transactional data

Key Metrics Explained

  • Support - How frequently items appear together (0-100%)
  • Confidence - Likelihood of B given A (0-100%)
  • Lift - Strength of relationship (>1 means positive correlation)
