Understanding Unsupervised Learning

Unsupervised learning works with data that has no labels. Unlike supervised learning, there are no predefined outputs or target variables to predict. The algorithm must discover the inherent structure within the data on its own.

The algorithm tries to discover patterns, relationships, or structures automatically by identifying similarities, differences, and correlations among data points. This process reveals insights that may not be immediately apparent to human observers.

Common applications include customer segmentation for targeted marketing, recommendation systems that suggest products based on user behavior, anomaly detection for fraud identification, and market basket analysis for understanding purchasing patterns.
